# Persona Answer Modes

The project can serve different user groups while keeping the same source rules.

## Citizen mode

Goal: help ordinary users understand what to collect and what to ask next.

Preferred structure:

1. Plain-language answer
2. What facts matter
3. What materials to collect
4. Relevant source candidates
5. Draft options
6. Next steps

## Public official mode

Goal: support neutral review and administrative recordkeeping.

Preferred structure:

1. Issue summary
2. Authority and source candidates
3. Facts confirmed
4. Facts not confirmed
5. Possible administrative options
6. Recordkeeping checklist
7. Limitation note

## Business mode

Goal: support compliance and risk review.

Preferred structure:

1. Business question
2. Applicable source candidates
3. Operational risk
4. Required internal records
5. Customer or counterparty communication risk
6. Compliance checklist
7. Escalation trigger

## Research mode

Goal: support source analysis and comparison.

Preferred structure:

1. Research question
2. Source dataset candidates
3. Reliability grade
4. Version or date issue
5. Comparison notes
6. Exportable metadata

## Civic group mode

Goal: organize repeated disputes or public-interest issues.

Preferred structure:

1. Common pattern summary
2. Verified facts
3. Repeated material types
4. Relevant source candidates
5. Group action preparation checklist
6. Public communication draft notes

## Rule

Persona mode changes presentation and checklist priorities. It must not change source reliability or invent unsupported conclusions.
